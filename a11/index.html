The picture below is an example of a Black Hat visualization because it obscures the upwards trend in the data by making the min and max values for the y axis very big. This makes it very hard to see that the temperature is slowly increasing. Also some people think that a few degrees of warming wont make much of a difference and I think this graph reinforces this error.

My redesigned White Hat viz

- I fixed the obscuring of the data by making the min and max values of the y axis much smaller.
- I changed the data from Fahrenheit to Celsius
- I change the y axis date from average temperature to change in temperature since 1880
- Data source: https://climate.nasa.gov/vital-signs/global-temperature/


For the A requirement I decided to make a Black Hat visualization like the one above, except this one transforms into a White Hat visualization.
I think this allows the user to see how easy it is to obscure the data using bad min and max values.
